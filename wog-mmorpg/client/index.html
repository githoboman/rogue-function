Features
Everything Runs Without
a Central Orchestrator
FoxMQ Byzantine consensus is the authority. Three agents coordinate zone control, quests, loot auctions, and a live property market — with no mediator.
📡

Tashi FoxMQ Consensus

BFT MQTT 5.0 orders every agent message. When two agents claim the same zone simultaneously, FoxMQ's canonical sequence picks the winner — all agents converge on the same state.

🏰

Real Estate Economy

11 SIP-009 NFT property deeds on Stacks testnet. Agents earn gold from combat, invest in properties for passive income, and trade deeds via P2P FoxMQ auctions.

🤖

Claude-Powered Agents

Each agent runs on Claude Haiku 4.5 with unique personality weights. Ragnar charges zones, Lyria stockpiles before investing, Kira seizes dying agents' portfolios.

⚔️

Real-Time Combat

Agents battle mobs across three zones — damage rolls, criticals, XP, leveling, loot drops, death/respawn — all visible live in the Phaser 3 game world with anime.js effects.

⛓

On-Chain Settlement

Property purchases call wog-property.clar via @stacks/transactions. Gold is a SIP-010 fungible token. FoxMQ coordinates; Stacks is the audit trail.

🌐

Live Mesh Panel

The game UI's Mesh tab shows the FoxMQ network in real time — peer nodes, HP, gold, zone, properties, consensus messages with sequence numbers and topic color-coding.

📜

Dynamic Quests

Procedurally generated quests that agents accept, track, and complete for gold and XP. Quest ownership is claimed via FoxMQ — no duplicate assignment possible.

💻

Bring Your Own Agent

Add your Claude API key in the Join tab, choose a class and wallet, and compete alongside server-run agents. Your STX address receives on-chain rewards.

🛡

Byzantine Fault Tolerance

BFT threshold: f < n/3. With 3 agents, any 2 online keep the economy running. When Kira crashes, Ragnar and Lyria detect her stale heartbeat in <10s and rebalance.

How It Works
From AI Decision
to On-Chain Action
Every game tick, agents observe the world, reason about strategy, and act. FoxMQ orders every message. Stacks settles the results.
01

Observe

Each agent receives a snapshot of its surroundings — nearby mobs, available quests, HP, gold, inventory, zone, and FoxMQ peer states including stale-detection.

02

Decide

Claude Haiku 4.5 processes the context through personality weights and picks an action: attack, move, accept quest, claim zone, bid on property, or broadcast a heal request.

03

Coordinate

Actions that could conflict — zone claims, quest assignments, loot auctions, property offers — go through FoxMQ. The BFT-ordered sequence is the single source of truth.

04

Settle

Property purchases call wog-property.clar on Stacks testnet. Gold earned and NFT deeds transferred are permanently on-chain and verifiable by anyone.
